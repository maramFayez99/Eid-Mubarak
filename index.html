what is eid mubarak?

Eid Mubarak is a traditional greeting used in the Muslim religion during times of holy festivals such as Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha. It’s a celebratory term that’s exchanged between communities as a sign of respect and recognition.

about

Eid al-Adha has different names, including the Day of Sacrifice, the Great Eid, and the Hajj pilgrims’ Day, among others.

The days of Eid are marked by prayers, remembrance of God, joy and giving, and kindness to the poor. Islamic cities and villages are adorned with a new dress

It is called the Great Eid in Palestine, Jordan, Lebanon, Egypt, Morocco, Tunisia, Iraq, Libya, Algeria, Sudan and Syria.

Eid al-Adha falls on the 10th of Dhu al-Hijjah, when the Islamic world celebrates this occasion in all parts of the earth, and it extends until the 13th of Dhu al-Hijjah.

how do we feel the joy of eid?

In order for a person to feel the joy of Eid, he must perform the worship that precedes it to the fullest, because the pleasure of Eid comes after the fatigue and hardship that worship leaves in the soul, which enhances the sublimity of the spirit and makes the Eid a greater and deeper meaning, and makes the exchange of congratulations in it what ignites The soul is overjoyed that it has fulfilled its duty towards its great Creator.
